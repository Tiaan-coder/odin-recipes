Bobotie

A dish dating back to 1609!

Ingredients

  • 1kg beef mince (lean)
  • 2 slices white bread, soaked in water, pressed to a pulp
  • 250 ml pitless raisins
  • 3 onions, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 15 ml fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 30 ml soft brown sugar
  • Salt & pepper

    • Dry spices

  • 15 ml mild curry powder
  • 5 ml ground turmeric
  • 5 ml ground coriander
  • 5 ml ground ginger
  • 2,5 ml ground cumin

    • Wet ingredients

  • 30 ml vegetable oil
  • 30 ml Worcestershire sauce
  • 30 ml lemon juice
  • 30 ml apricot jam
  • 60 ml fruit chutney
  • 30 ml tomato paste

    • For the custard

  • 500 ml milk
  • 4 eggs
  • 4 bay leaves
  • Salt & pepper

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the oil and fry the onions until soft and starting to brown lightly.
  2. Add the garlic, ginger and dry spices and fry for another minute until the bottom of the pot goes dry and sticky.
  3. Add the beef bit by bit, breaking up any lumpy pieces. Fry, stirring, until it just starts to change colour from pink to light brown – before adding more meat. The meat shouldn’t brown too much. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  4. Add the wet ingredients and raisins and give it a good stir.
  5. Reduce heat to a simmer, cover and cook for 30 minutes, stirring often and taking care not to burn the bottom of the pot. Add a touch of water if the mixture is too dry. In the meantime, pre-heat oven to 180 C.
  6. Prepare the custard topping: mix the milk and eggs and season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
  7. When the bobotie is ready, transfer it to a large oven-proof baking dish and flatten the surface with a spatula.
  8. Press the bay leaves into the bobotie, then pour the custard mixture over the top.
  9. Carefully place in the oven and bake for 40 minutes until the custard is set.
  10. Remove from oven and let it stand for 10 minutes before serving.
